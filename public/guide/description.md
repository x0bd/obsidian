# Project: Obsidian – Quantitative Command Center

## 1. Project Concept: The High-Frequency Nerve Center

Obsidian is not a standard financial application; it is a mission-critical "Nerve Center" designed for the extreme data density of high-frequency quantitative trading. It is built to mimic the psychological state of a Mission Control terminal—where every flickering pixel represents a live market event, and the UI must communicate complex telemetry without overwhelming the pilot (the trader).

---

## 2. Visual Architecture & Layout

The interface utilizes a **Modular Grid System** that rejects the "whitespace-heavy" trends of modern web design in favor of **Information Density**.

- **The Command Grid:** A dense, multi-panel dashboard where every square inch of screen real estate is utilized for telemetry.
- **Kinetic Elements:** Subtle scanline overlays and 2px "circuit-trace" borders give the UI a mechanical, hardware-integrated feel.
- **Data Motion:** Real-time ticker feeds do not simply "update"—they pulse. The UI uses kinetic motion to highlight whale trades and volatility spikes, ensuring the eye is drawn to critical events instantly.

---

## 3. Color Palette: "The Solar Flare in the Void"

The color story is built on the concept of high-energy data cutting through an obsidian vacuum.

- **Primary Base (The Void):** `#050505` (True Obsidian). A deep, non-reflective black that reduces eye strain during 12-hour shifts.
- **Primary Accent (Cyber-Orange):** `#FF8C00`. Used for active data streams, executable buttons, and primary telemetry.
- **Alert State (Burn-Orange):** `#FF4500`. Reserved for high-volatility events, order executions, and system warnings.
- **Secondary Phosphor:** Subtle `#1A1A1A` for grid lines and `#333333` for inactive telemetry to maintain a clear visual hierarchy.

---

## 4. Typography & Symbolism

The typography is strictly functional, pulling from aerospace and terminal heritage.

- **Primary Typeface:** `JetBrains Mono` or `Roboto Mono`. Monospaced fonts ensure that price columns never shift or "jump" when numbers change, maintaining perfect vertical alignment.
- **Hierarchy:** - **Header 1 (System Status):** Small-caps, wide-spaced monospaced text for a "military-grade" feel.
  - **Data Points:** Bold, high-contrast numerals with a subtle outer glow (0.5px) to mimic phosphor screens.
- **Iconography:** Minimalist, wireframe-style icons from the `Lucide` library, stripped of all rounded corners to maintain a sharp, industrial edge.

---

## 5. The Mood: Industrial Urgency

The "Mood" of Obsidian is defined as **Aggressive Intelligence**. It is designed to feel like an expensive piece of proprietary hardware found on a dark-site trading floor.

- **Atmosphere:** Dark, focused, and high-stakes.
- **Vibe:** "Hardware-First." The UI feels like it was programmed directly into a terminal rather than rendered in a browser.
- **User Experience:** Every interaction is accompanied by "tactile" visual feedback—buttons don't just click; they "engage" with high-speed transitions and orange phosphor glows.
